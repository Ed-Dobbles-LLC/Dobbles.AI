# I Had a Problem. So I Built a Podcast.

As a C-suite analytics exec, I need to stay sharp on AI, data strategy, market shifts — the topics that show up in board conversations and client pitches. But I don't have time to sit and read 20 articles a day to find the three that matter.

What I do have time for: listening. I already listen to Hard Fork and The Daily when I drive or work out. Podcasts fit into how I actually operate — they let me learn while I do other things.

But here's the gap: I can't find a podcast on the specific thing I need to know about by Thursday.

So I built one.

I built an AI-powered tool where I give it a topic — one-off or recurring — and it does the rest. It researches the subject, verifies what it finds, writes a two-person debate script, records the audio, and drops the finished episode into my Apple Podcasts feed.

I open my podcast app. The episode is there. I learn while I drive. Done.

No new app to check. No new habit to build. No dashboard to log into. It fits into a workflow I already have.

That last part is the whole point.

Most technology introductions fail — not because the tech isn't good, but because they ask people to change how they work. I wrote my doctoral dissertation on exactly this: why advanced analytics tools fail to get adopted inside organizations. The answer almost always comes back to the same thing. The tool didn't fit the user's actual workflow.

Strategy before tools. Problem before solution. Behavior before interface.
