# 10X Nothing Is Still Nothing

Let's be honest about something most analytics leaders won't say out loud.

For 25 years, the most important skill in this job wasn't statistics or storytelling. It was triage. Deciding whose request was worth the capacity and whose wasn't.

I had a line I stole from my old boss Dave: 10X nothing is still nothing. A small brand, a small geography, a genuinely interesting question — didn't matter. If it couldn't move the number that mattered, it didn't get the analyst.

I wasn't wrong. I was just operating inside a broken system.

AI breaks that constraint. Which means analytics functions are about to face a fork in the road.

**Option one:** maintain current capacity, radically increase output, and finally deliver the strategic value analytics has always promised but never had the bandwidth to reach.

**Option two:** the CFO notices the productivity gain, pockets the savings, cuts the team, and asks you to deliver the same work with half the people. Then half again.

That's not a hypothetical. That's how enterprise cost optimization works. And analytics leaders who don't get ahead of this conversation are going to find someone else is having it for them.

The leaders who win this moment won't win it by walking into the CFO's office with a plan. They'll win it by walking in with results already on the board. Cobbled together if necessary. Through shadow IT if necessary.

Apologize later if necessary. Because a defensible budget line isn't a roadmap. It's a track record.
