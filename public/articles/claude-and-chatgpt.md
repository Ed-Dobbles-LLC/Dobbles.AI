# Claude and ChatGPT Hate Each Other. Good. I Use That to My Advantage.

Claude is my primary thinking partner. Claude Code is my doer. But when we hit a wall, I ask Claude to write a briefing on what we're stuck on — then build a prompt I take to the competition for a consult.

Depending on how stuck I am, ChatGPT either answers directly or I invoke deep research mode. Full investigation.

Then Claude reviews what comes back, takes what's useful, and disregards the rest. It doesn't defer. It exercises judgment — like a senior analyst reviewing outside work.

I've written this into my standing Claude doctrine. Escalation path, rules of engagement, defined roles. It's not a model preference. It's a system.

Thinking partner. Doer. Outside consult. Judgment layer.

That's a team, not a tool.

Most leaders are still debating which AI to pick. The ones who win are building governance around how the tools work together.

The model isn't the strategy. The system is.
